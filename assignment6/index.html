A Short Look into Deaf Education in American History

Deaf education pre-1880 was a positive time for sign language. Fingerspelling was recognized as the first language of traditional sign language in 1878, and students were being taught using sign language. It was the most beneficial method of teaching at the time. Post-1880, however, there was a shift in the idea of how deaf education should be executed - in 1880, the International Congress on the Education of the Deaf in Milan, Italy, decreed that oralism was the only method that should be utilized in Deaf schools. This brought upon a dark age for Deaf people, making education a struggle and leaving some students left in the dust with no real language and thus, no real education. In current years, some aspects of oralism are utilized, but the primary communication method used in Deaf education is sign language. It is accepted as a full language, and is being taught in non-Deaf schools as a foreign language. This radical change bettered Deaf education and provided more accessibility to students, ensuring Deaf people get the education they need and deserve.
